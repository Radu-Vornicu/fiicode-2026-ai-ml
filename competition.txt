Overview
In this competition, participants are challenged to build a binary classification model that predicts whether a client of a Portuguese banking institution will subscribe to a term deposit following a direct marketing campaign.

The dataset is based on real-world data from phone-based marketing campaigns, where clients were contacted one or multiple times as part of an outreach effort. Each record captures a snapshot of a client’s demographic profile, financial status, and interaction history with previous marketing campaigns.

The objective is to leverage this information to accurately identify clients who are most likely to subscribe to the offered product. A reliable prediction model can help banks improve the efficiency of their marketing campaigns by focusing efforts on high-potential customers, reducing unnecessary contact costs, and increasing overall conversion rates.

Participants will train their models using the provided training dataset, which includes the ground truth labels, and generate predictions for an unlabeled test dataset. Submissions will be evaluated against a hidden ground truth using a predefined evaluation metric, and performance will be ranked on a leaderboard.

This challenge focuses on solving a realistic business problem involving customer behavior prediction, class imbalance, and generalization to unseen data.

Evaluation
Evaluation
Submissions are evaluated using the Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic Curve (ROC-AUC).

The ROC-AUC metric measures the ability of the model to distinguish between clients who subscribe to the term deposit and those who do not.

Predictions should be probabilities between 0 and 1 indicating the likelihood that a client subscribes to the term deposit.

Dataset Description
This dataset, originally introduced in [Moro 2014], contains data from direct marketing campaigns conducted by a Portuguese banking institution. The campaigns aimed to promote a bank term deposit and were carried out primarily through phone calls.

Each row in the dataset represents information about a client and their interaction history during a marketing campaign. In many cases, multiple phone contacts were made to the same client over time. The goal of these repeated interactions was to determine whether the client would ultimately subscribe to the offered term deposit.

The target variable (Subscribed) indicates the final outcome of the campaign: 1 — the client subscribed to the term deposit 0 — the client did not subscribe

Using the provided demographic, financial, and campaign-related features, participants are challenged to build a predictive model that accurately identifies which clients are most likely to subscribe to the term deposit. Such a model can help financial institutions optimize marketing strategies, reduce operational costs, and improve campaign effectiveness.

References:

[Moro 2014] Moro, S., Rita, P., & Cortez, P. (2014). Bank Marketing [Dataset]. UCI Machine Learning Repository. https://doi.org/10.24432/C5K306.

Feature description
Input variables:
bank client data:
id (numeric)
age (numeric)
job : type of job (categorical: "admin.","unknown","unemployed","management","housemaid","entrepreneur","student", "blue-collar","self-employed","retired","technician","services")
marital : marital status (categorical: "married","divorced","single"; note: "divorced" means divorced or widowed)
education (categorical: "unknown","secondary","primary","tertiary")
default: has credit in default? (binary: "yes","no")
balance: average yearly balance, in euros (numeric)
housing: has housing loan? (binary: "yes","no")
loan: has personal loan? (binary: "yes","no")
related with the last contact of the current campaign:
contact: contact communication type (categorical: "unknown","telephone","cellular")
day: last contact day of the month (numeric)
month: last contact month of year (categorical: "jan", "feb", "mar", …, "nov", "dec")
duration: last contact duration, in seconds (numeric)
other attributes:
campaign: number of contacts performed during this campaign and for this client (numeric, includes last contact)
pdays: number of days that passed by after the client was last contacted from a previous campaign (numeric, -1 means client was not previously contacted)
previous: number of contacts performed before this campaign and for this client (numeric)
poutcome: outcome of the previous marketing campaign (categorical: "unknown","other","failure","success")
Output variable (desired target):
Subscribed: has the client subscribed a term deposit? (numeric: 1 - means 'yes', 0 - means 'no')
